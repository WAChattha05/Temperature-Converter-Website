About Temperature Conversions

Temperature conversion is the process of changing a temperature value from one unit of measurement to another. It's a common task in various fields, including science, engineering, and everyday life.

There are several temperature scales, with Celsius (℃), Kelvin (K), Fahrenheit (℉), and Rankine (°R) being some of the most commonly used ones. Each scale has its own unique characteristics and formulas for conversion.

For example, to convert from Celsius to Kelvin, you can use the formula:

            Kelvin = Celsius + 273.15

And to convert from Fahrenheit to Celsius, you can use:

            Celsius = (Fahrenheit - 32) * 5/9
